Equipment Inventory and Usage Guide

Core Workstation Setup

Each station has a **Breadboard** for circuit assembly, a **DC Regulated Power Supply** (+5V for TTL ICs), **Connecting Wires**, and kits containing common **Digital ICs** (7400, 7404, 7476, etc.).

Primary Workstation Tool List

  • **Breadboard:** Non-soldering circuit assembly platform.
  • **DC Regulated Power Supply:** Provides stable +5V DC (TTL Standard).
  • **Connecting Wires:** Complete set of jumper wires for connections.
  • **Digital IC Kits:** Contains common TTL logic families (74XX series).

Key Measuring and Visualization Instruments

Accurate measurement is critical. You will use the **CRO** to visualize and measure digital waveforms and timing. The **Digital Multimeter (DMM)** is used for static voltage checks and continuity. The **Function Generator** provides stable clock signals (square waves) for sequential circuit testing.

Essential Visualization and Measurement Tools

  • **CRO (Cathode Ray Oscilloscope):** Observes and measures digital waveforms (timing, voltage).
  • **Digital Multimeter (DMM):** Measures DC voltage and checks circuit continuity.
  • **Function Generator:** Generates stable square waves (clock signals) for sequential testing.
  • **Logic Analyzer:** (If available) Captures and displays multiple digital signals for complex debugging.

Specialized training tools, such as Experimental T.V.s, are reserved for advanced application experiments. Specific instruction on their use will be provided by staff.

Lab Procedures and Experiments

In-Lab Workflow

  1. **Pre-Lab:** Read the procedure and complete all pre-lab calculations (Staff will check readiness).
  2. **Assembly:** Build your circuit neatly on the breadboard.
  3. **Power Check:** **MUST** get verification from staff before applying power.
  4. **Testing:** Verify circuit function using the CRO and DMM.
  5. **Troubleshooting:** Verify Power, Connections, and IC Orientation in that order if the circuit fails.
  6. **Post-Lab:** Turn off all equipment, disassemble the circuit, clean your station, and submit your report.
