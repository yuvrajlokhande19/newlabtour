Safety Rules and Emergency Procedures

Strict adherence to safety guidelines is required for all personnel.

General Safety Protocol

  • Storage: Store bags in designated areas to prevent tripping hazards.
  • No Food/Drink: Absolutely no food or drinks are allowed on the workbenches.
  • Reporting: Report faulty equipment, damaged cords, or any injury immediately to lab staff.
  • Tidiness: Ensure your work area is clean and tidy before leaving.

Electrical Safety and Emergency

  • Inspect Cords: Always inspect power cords (CRO, DMM) for damage before use.
  • Check Polarity: Double-check Vcc and GND polarity on all ICs before applying power (+5V DC).

Equipment Inventory and Usage Guide

Core Workstation Setup

Each station has a Breadboard for circuit assembly, a DC Regulated Power Supply (+5V for TTL ICs), Connecting Wires, and kits containing common Digital ICs (7400, 7404, 7476, Multimeter.).

Primary Workstation Tool List

  • Breadboard: Non-soldering circuit assembly platform.
  • DC Regulated Power Supply: Provides stable +5V DC (TTL Standard). Never exceed 5V for TTL circuits.
  • Connecting Wires: Complete set of jumper wires for connections.
  • Digital IC Kits: Contains common TTL logic families (74XX series).
  • Multimeter: For Measuring Components.
  • Soldering System: For Soldering Eletronic Component

Key Measuring and Visualization Instruments

Accurate measurement is critical. You will use the CRO (Cathod Ray Oscillator) to visualize and measure digital waveforms and timing. The Digital Multimeter (DMM) is used for static voltage checks and continuity. The Function Generator provides stable clock signals (square waves) for sequential circuit testing.

Essential Visualization and Measurement Tools

  • CRO (Cathode Ray Oscilloscope): Observes and measures digital waveforms (timing, voltage, frequency).
  • Digital Multimeter (DMM): Measures DC voltage, current, and checks circuit continuity.
  • Function Generator: Generates stable square waves (clock signals) for sequential testing.
  • Logic Analyzer: (If available) Captures and displays multiple digital signals for complex debugging.

Lab Procedures and Experiments

In-Lab Workflow Checklist

  1. Pre-Lab: Read the procedure and complete all pre-lab calculations (Staff will check readiness).
  2. Assembly: Build your circuit neatly on the breadboard, optimizing for shortest wire lengths.
  3. Power Check: MUST get verification from staff before applying power (Vcc and GND check).
  4. Testing: Verify circuit function using the CRO and DMM. Record observations immediately.
  5. Troubleshooting: Verify Power (voltage), Connections (continuity), and IC Orientation (notch) in that order if the circuit fails.
  6. Post-Lab: Turn off all equipment, disassemble the circuit, clean your station, and submit your report.
